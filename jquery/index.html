20th Century Boys

This time Urasawa chose to make a much less grounded story. 20th Century Boys is a longer story that spans generations focusing on an everyman named Kenji whose normal life gets taken away as he is forced to deal with a Japanese cult that plans on taking over the world. This is very much a character driven story and it wouldn't work if Urasawa wasn't capable of creating a large cast of compelling and interesting characters. If you want to check out a story full of political intrigue, conspiracies, and extensive character growth then this is for you.

Manben

While this isn't exactly a manga, Manben is a documentary style series which Urasawa hosts. Every episode, he interviews a different manga artist and gets their perspective. If you're even slightly interested in how the manga industry works, the different tools artists use to draw, or would like to hear different ideas on how to convey emotions and ideas visually then I recommend giving this series a watch. While Manben is not in English, translated and subtitled episodes are quite easy to find.

Monster

One of the first manga by Urasawa I've read, Monster to this day leaves a strong impression on me. This is a suspenseful mystery story about an idealistic Japanese doctor Tenma working in Europe who is framed for murder and is forced on the run to try and expose the real killer. This is what proved to me that Urasawa is capable of writing some of the most compelling and interesting characters in the medium with how he challenges Tenma's morals throughout this journey. This is definitely a must read if you are interested in Urasawa's work.

Pluto

While one of his shorter works, Pluto is easily one of the best manga I've ever read. Urasawa shows how versatitle he is as in artist by being able to draw expressive humans alongside hyper detailed complex mechanical parts. Pluto is staged in a post-war world where technology has advanced far enough to make sentient AI in the form of robots. However, robots are treated like second class citizens tend to be abused. The plot follows a robot detective named Gesicht as he attempts to find the suspect behind a string of robot deaths. Full of high stakes and tension, Pluto is a very emotionally charged story about making peace in a world that was once devastated by war. It would be a disservice to say anymore as I highly encourage you to read this story.
