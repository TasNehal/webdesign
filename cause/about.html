About Us

Our Goal

Our organization provides support for local animal shelters. We want to provide as many homes as we can for stray animals and educate the public on the virtues of pet adoption.

The Problem

As of now there is an estimated count of 6 million animals being housed in animal shelters. Unfortunately, animal shelters don't have the resources to support all of them and 1.5 million shelter animals end up getting euthanized yearly. This does not even account for the 70 million stray animals without any homes or support. We want to cut down on these numbers as much as possible.

Why You Should Care

Many of these stray animals die to starvation or preventable disease which can impact living conditions within a city. Sometimes shelters abuse their animals, neglecting them of the resources they need too. Pet vendors that market and sell specific breeds of animals are counterproductive to the problem of the population of animals being neglected and often purposefully breed animals with genetic defects.

How You Can Help

The solution to these problems is to advocate for adoption. These poor neglected creatures deserve love and care too. By adopting a stray animal or by contacting your local animal shelter, you can lower the burden on shelters and cut down on the number of starving and diseased animals that are forced to live in the streets and find a loving companion in return. By buying pets directly from animal breeders and vendors instead of adopting, you only add directly to the problem.
